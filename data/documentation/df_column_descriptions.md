| Column Name                               | Description (Revised for Your Dataset)                                                                                                  | Units                               | Notes / Intuitive Explanation                                                                                                                          |
| ----------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Sample_ID**                             | Unique identifier for each individual observation or record (e.g., a coral reef monitoring sample).                                     | —                                   | Used to uniquely identify rows; not meaningful for analysis.                                                                                           |
| **Site_ID**                               | Identifier for the geographical site where the observation was recorded.                                                                | —                                   | All samples with the same Site_ID belong to the same reef location.                                                                                    |
| **ClimSST**                               | *Climatological* Sea Surface Temperature — long-term average temperature at that location, based on historical weekly means.            | **Kelvin**                          | Represents the “typical” temperature at that site; a baseline for anomaly calculations.                                                                |
| **Temperature_Kelvin**                    | Sea surface temperature at the sampling time.                                                                                           | **Kelvin**                          | Convert to °C by subtracting 273.15 if needed.                                                                                                         |
| **Temperature_Mean**                      | Mean sea surface temperature over the study period.                                                                                     | **Kelvin**                          | Reflects average ocean temperature conditions across time.                                                                                             |
| **Temperature_Minimum**                   | Minimum observed temperature during the study period.                                                                                   | **Kelvin**                          | Lowest recorded water temperature at that site.                                                                                                        |
| **Temperature_Maximum**                   | Maximum observed temperature during the study period.                                                                                   | **Kelvin**                          | Highest recorded water temperature at that site.                                                                                                       |
| **Temperature_Kelvin_Standard_Deviation** | Standard deviation of temperature measurements (how much temperatures fluctuate).                                                       | **Kelvin**                          | Higher values = greater temperature variability.                                                                                                       |
| **Windspeed**                             | Average windspeed at the site.                                                                                                          | **meters per second (m/s)**         | Helps indicate exposure and potential cooling of surface waters.                                                                                       |
| **SSTA**                                  | **Sea Surface Temperature Anomaly** — the difference between the current weekly temperature (observed SST) and the long-term climatological average (ClimSST).       | **Kelvin (temperature difference)** | Positive values mean the sea was warmer than normal. This is key for detecting heat stress linked to coral bleaching.                                  |
| **SSTA_Standard_Deviation**               | Standard deviation of SSTA values.                                                                                                      | **Kelvin**                          | Indicates how much the anomaly fluctuates over time.                                                                                                   |
| **SSTA_Mean**                             | Mean anomaly over the observation period.                                                                                               | **Kelvin**                          | Average deviation from normal conditions; a positive mean suggests sustained warming.                                                                  |
| **SSTA_Minimum**                          | Minimum (most negative) temperature anomaly observed.                                                                                   | **Kelvin**                          | Indicates the largest cool deviation relative to normal.                                                                                               |
| **SSTA_Maximum**                          | Maximum temperature anomaly observed.                                                                                                   | **Kelvin**                          | Indicates the largest warm deviation relative to normal.                                                                                               |
| **SSTA_Frequency**                        | Number of weeks in the previous 52-week period where the SSTA ≥ 1°C.                                                                    | **weeks**                           | Essentially: “How often was the sea at least 1°C warmer than usual this year?” Higher = more frequent stress.                                          |
| **SSTA_Frequency_Standard_Deviation**     | Standard deviation of SSTA_Frequency across time.                                                                                       | **weeks**                           | Measures how consistent or variable that frequency is.                                                                                                 |
| **SSTA_FrequencyMax**                     | Maximum number of heat-stress weeks recorded in any year.                                                                               | **weeks**                           | The most extreme year in terms of sustained warmth.                                                                                                    |
| **SSTA_FrequencyMean**                    | Mean number of heat-stress weeks per year.                                                                                              | **weeks**                           | Average annual frequency of moderate warming events.                                                                                                   |
| **SSTA_DHW**                              | **Degree Heating Weeks** based on SSTA: A measure of accumulated heat stress, calculated by summing weekly anomalies ≥ 1°C over the past 12 weeks. | **degree-weeks (°C·weeks)**         | A core coral-bleaching index — higher values mean more accumulated heat stress. Values >4 °C·weeks often indicate bleaching risk.                      |
| **SSTA_DHW_Standard_Deviation**           | Standard deviation of SSTA_DHW over time.                                                                                               | **degree-weeks**                    | Reflects variability in accumulated heat stress between years.                                                                                         |
| **SSTA_DHWMax**                           | Maximum SSTA_DHW recorded.                                                                                                              | **degree-weeks**                    | The highest level of accumulated thermal stress at that site.                                                                                          |
| **SSTA_DHWMean**                          | Mean SSTA_DHW across all time periods.                                                                                                  | **degree-weeks**                    | The typical thermal stress load over time.                                                                                                             |
| **TSA**                                   | **Thermal Stress Anomaly** — The difference between the current weekly temperature (SST) and the *maximum* climatological SST for that location.                     | **Kelvin (temperature difference)** | TSA focuses on extreme heat relative to the hottest expected “normal.” Positive TSA = temperatures exceeding local maxima, often triggering bleaching. |
| **TSA_Standard_Deviation**                | Standard deviation of TSA over time.                                                                                                    | **Kelvin**                          | Indicates variability of extreme heat anomalies.                                                                                                       |
| **TSA_Minimum**                           | Minimum TSA (coolest deviation).                                                                                                        | **Kelvin**                          | Negative values show unusually cool events relative to climatological maxima.                                                                          |
| **TSA_Maximum**                           | Maximum TSA (strongest warm deviation).                                                                                                 | **Kelvin**                          | Indicates the most intense temperature stress observed.                                                                                                |
| **TSA_Mean**                              | Mean TSA over the observation period.                                                                                                   | **Kelvin**                          | Average level of extreme heat relative to expected seasonal peaks.                                                                                     |
| **TSA_Frequency**                         | Number of weeks in the last 52 weeks where TSA ≥ 1°C.                                                                                   | **weeks**                           | Measures how often the water exceeded its typical warm threshold — frequent exceedances mean sustained stress.                                         |
| **TSA_Frequency_Standard_Deviation**      | Standard deviation of TSA_Frequency.                                                                                                    | **weeks**                           | Indicates year-to-year variability in how often extreme heat occurs.                                                                                   |
| **TSA_FrequencyMax**                      | Maximum number of TSA heat-stress weeks recorded in any year.                                                                           | **weeks**                           | The single most stressful year recorded.                                                                                                               |
| **TSA_FrequencyMean**                     | Mean number of TSA heat-stress weeks per year.                                                                                          | **weeks**                           | Typical annual frequency of extreme thermal stress events.                                                                                             |
| **TSA_DHW**                               | Degree Heating Weeks based on TSA — accumulated weeks where TSA ≥ 1°C over the previous 12 weeks.                                       | **degree-weeks (°C·weeks)**         | Another measure of cumulative heat exposure; high values indicate prolonged or repeated exposure above local heat thresholds.                          |
| **TSA_DHW_Standard_Deviation**            | Standard deviation of TSA_DHW over time.                                                                                                | **degree-weeks**                    | Variability of cumulative heat exposure.                                                                                                               |
| **TSA_DHWMax**                            | Maximum TSA_DHW recorded.                                                                                                               | **degree-weeks**                    | Peak cumulative stress observed.                                                                                                                       |
| **TSA_DHWMean**                           | Mean TSA_DHW across the entire period.                                                                                                  | **degree-weeks**                    | Average accumulated heat exposure.                                                                                                                     |
| **Depth_m**                               | Depth of the sampling location.                                                                                                         | **meters (m)**                      | Deeper reefs may be more protected from temperature swings and light stress.                                                                           |
| **Distance_to_Shore**                     | Distance from the sampling site to the nearest coastline.                                                                               | **meters (m)**                      | Proxy for how “offshore” or “coastal” a reef is; affects exposure and turbidity.                                                                       |
| **Exposure**                              | Categorical indicator of wave/wind exposure — values: **“exposed”**, **“sheltered”**, or **“sometimes.”**                               | —                                   | “Exposed” sites face prevailing winds; “sheltered” are protected; “sometimes” are partially exposed seasonally.                                        |
| **Turbidity**                             | Light attenuation coefficient (Kd490), measuring how much light is scattered or absorbed by particles in the water.                     | **m⁻¹**                             | Higher = murkier water; can protect corals from light stress but reduce photosynthesis.                                                                |
| **Cyclone_Frequency**                     | Number of cyclone events between 1964–2014 near the site.                                                                               | —                                   | Historical storm frequency; high values imply more physical disturbance.                                                                               |
| **Percent_Bleached**                      | Percentage of corals at the site observed to be bleached.                                                                               | **percent (%)**                     | The main target variable — indicates coral health (higher percentage = worse coral health).                                                            |
